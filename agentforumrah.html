Cost Efficiency (CPL)

The campaign was highly efficient, achieving a Cost Per Lead (CPL) of $0.17, beating the $0.33 target by over 48%. (Note: Costs based on Indonesian market rates).

Geographical Lead Distribution

A key goal was to reach untapped markets outside major cities. While the core Jabodetabek (Greater Jakarta) area provided a strong 38% of registrants, the campaign successfully penetrated diverse new markets across Indonesia (e.g., North Maluku, Papua), proving the model's scalability.

Registrant Experience

A majority (65%) of registrants had little to no prior sales experience, identifying a key training need.

Registrant Ambition

Despite inexperience, 39% were highly ambitious, aiming for earnings over $1,300/month.

Key Lesson Learned üí°

Lead generation was a success, but the data revealed a significant "motivation-experience gap." The key challenge is now conversion, not just acquisition. Future strategy must focus on structured training to manage expectations, reduce churn, and implement sales performance tracking (ROI).
