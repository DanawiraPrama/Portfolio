Campaign Narrative: Open Letter to Hana Bank

The campaign began with an open letter delivered by the Kpop4planet community and BigBang G-Dragon fandom to Hana Bank Indonesia. The letter urged the bank to stop financing Harita's nickel project on Obi Island, which was deemed environmentally damaging.

Strategic Insight:

The spike from 0 to 44 mentions during the analysis period indicates a coordinated campaign, not organic virality. This demonstrates the organizational power of K-Pop fandom when mobilized for social causes.

The campaign successfully bridged two seemingly disconnected worlds: Pop Culture Fandom and Corporate Responsibility. This unique perspective made the story compelling for mainstream media journalists, creating a significant multiplier effect.

Sentiment Analysis: 100% Positive

100% positive sentiment in a campaign involving financial and mining issues is a rare phenomenon. Typically, such issues trigger debate and polarization of opinions.

Interpretation:

Consistent positive sentiment indicates an effective "us versus them" narrative. The campaign successfully created solidarity among K-Pop fans by presenting environmental issues as a shared struggle.

The absence of negative sentiment also indicates that the campaign has not attracted opposition attention or rejection, providing space for broader narrative expansion.

"Earned Media" Multiplier Effect

The most significant finding in this analysis is the disparity between Social Media Reach (2.3K) and Non-Social Media Reach (281K).

100x Multiplier Effect:

Fans ignited the fire (Social Media), but mainstream media poured gasoline on it (News). The 100:1 ratio demonstrates the effectiveness of media relations strategy that resulted in exponential amplification.

Major outlets like Greeners.co, Suara Merdeka, Wartalampung, and dozens of other regional media picked up the story, providing validation and expanding reach to broader non-K-Pop audiences.
