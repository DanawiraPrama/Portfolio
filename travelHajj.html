Analysis & Lesson Learned

Market Context: Indonesia

Please note: This case study was conducted in the Indonesian market. The ad spend and CPL ($0.41) are specific to this region, which typically has lower media costs compared to North American or European markets. The strategy and percentage-based results (like CTR and funnel effectiveness) are universally applicable.

The data clearly shows the "Community Trust" funnel was a major success. The campaign beat the target CPL by 18%, achieving a final CPL of $0.41. Our overall link CTR of 1.01% is highly competitive with Meta's travel industry benchmarks.

The most significant finding was the clear performance gap between creatives. The 'Darussalam' (Poster & Carousel) series, featuring authentic testimonials, generated 70% of all leads (750). The 'Darussalam Poster' (video) was the top performer with 400 leads. In contrast, the more generic 'Adn Carousel' generated only 100 leads.

Benchmark vs. Meta Standards

To contextualize these results, we compared them against Meta's industry benchmarks for travel:

  • Link CTR (Click-Through Rate): Our campaign's overall link CTR was 1.01%. This is right in line with the travel industry average, which typically hovers around 0.9% - 1.2%. This indicates our ads were relevant and visually appealing to the target audience.
  • Cost Per Lead (CPL): Our final CPL was $0.41. For a lead magnet (guide download) in the competitive travel sector, this is an exceptionally low and highly efficient result, far outperforming common CPL benchmarks which can often be several dollars.

Key Takeaway: For high-trust, high-ticket services like Hajj, authentic social proof (the 'Darussalam' video) dramatically outperforms generic corporate creative ('Adn'). Future campaigns should be social-proof-first.
